# Lean-AI — Financial Model

*Three-year plan · part-time founder constraint · all figures in EUR, net of VAT*

This model is intentionally conservative and built bottom-up from delivery capacity, not top-down from market size. It assumes the founder works **10–15 hrs/week** and protects time with a hard work-in-progress (WIP) limit.

---

## 1. Pricing (the engine)

| Offer | List price (from) | Effective founder hours | Implied effective rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Diagnostic | €4,500 | ~25–35 hrs | ~€130–180/hr |
| Automation Sprint | €12,000 | ~55–75 hrs | ~€160–220/hr |
| Operating Partner | €3,500/mo | ~12–18 hrs/mo | ~€195–290/hr |

> The effective rate climbs as offers move from one-off to recurring and as the template library matures — i.e. the same deliverable takes fewer hours over time. This is the core economic flywheel: **AI + reuse drive marginal delivery cost down while price holds.**

**Pricing principles**
- Value-based, never hourly on the invoice (hours shown above are internal only).
- The Diagnostic fee is **fully creditable** toward a Sprint — it is a paid sales process, not a profit centre.
- Retainers carry a **quarterly minimum** to make revenue predictable.
- Raise prices ~10–15% once at ≥80% capacity, before adding volume.

---

## 2. Capacity model (why the numbers are what they are)

At ~12 hrs/week ≈ ~600 productive hrs/year (allowing for holidays, admin, sales, and content). Hard WIP limit: **one active build at a time + up to ~2 retainers**, plus Diagnostics slotted between builds.

Rough annual delivery envelope at steady state:
- Diagnostics: ~30 hrs each → a handful per year between builds.
- Sprints: ~65 hrs each → ~5–7/year solo before a subcontractor is added.
- Retainers: ~15 hrs/mo each → ~2 sustainable solo; more with subcontractor leverage.

Subcontractor leverage (Year 2+) roughly doubles build/retainer capacity without doubling founder hours.

| Tooling (AI, automation, hosting) | –€4,200 | –€7,200 | –€10,800 |

Margin compresses (healthily) in Years 2–3 as subcontractor capacity is added to lift the founder's hour ceiling — trading some margin for growth and time-protection. Even at Year 3, margin stays strong because the business is senior-only and asset-light.

## 4. Scenarios

| Scenario | Year-1 revenue | Driver |
|---|---:|---|
| **Conservative** | ~€60–70k | Slow start, network only converts 1–2 Sprints, 1 retainer. |
| **Base** | ~€95k | As modelled above. |
| **Stretch** | ~€140k | Strong warm network, an early lighthouse client, 2–3 retainers by year-end. |

All three are achievable inside 10–15 hrs/week because the WIP limit caps simultaneous delivery; the difference is conversion rate and retainer attach, not founder hours.

---

## 5. Cash & break-even

- **Break-even is essentially immediate.** Fixed monthly cost in Year 1 is only a few hundred euros (tooling + admin). The first Diagnostic covers months of overhead.
- **No external funding required.** This is a bootstrapped, cash-positive service business. Do not raise money; raise prices.
- **Working-capital risk is low** but real for SMEs: invoice 50% up front on Sprints, 100% up front on Diagnostics, net-14 on retainers. Never start build work before the deposit clears.

---

## 6. Unit economics (per Sprint client, illustrative)

- Sprint revenue: €12,000; founder time ~65 hrs → ~€185/hr effective.
- If the client attaches a retainer (target ≥40% attach rate): +€3,500/mo recurring at ~€220/hr effective.
- **Customer lifetime value (LTV)** of a Sprint+retainer client over 12 months ≈ €12,000 + (€3,500 × ~9 avg months) ≈ **~€43,500**, at very low acquisition cost (warm network + content).

---

## 7. Tax & VAT note (general info, not advice)

- Decide VAT posture early: for B2B clients, charging 19% USt is usually neutral (they reclaim it) and avoids the Kleinunternehmer "small/cheap" signal. Model above is shown **net of VAT** either way.
- Set aside income tax provisions from day one (the side income stacks on top of salaried income and can push into higher marginal brackets — confirm with a Steuerberater).

---

## 8. The Year-3 decision point

At ~€320k revenue and a maturing retainer base, the founder faces a deliberate fork:
1. **Stay a premium side business** — cap growth, keep margin sky-high, protect the day job.
2. **Go full-time and scale** — convert subcontractors to a small senior team, push toward €750k–1M with the same productized model (cf. solo productized firms at $1.2M+).

This plan gets you to that choice with options open and no debt.
