## 1. Definition of Confidential Information
**"Confidential Information"** means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Discloser to the Recipient, in any form (oral, written, electronic, visual, or by access to systems), whether or not marked or designated as confidential, that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure. It includes, without limitation: business plans, strategies, pricing, financials, customer and supplier data, personal data, processes and operations, methods, frameworks, templates, prompts, AI-agent designs, source code and configurations, technical and product information, and the existence and content of the Parties' discussions.

## 2. Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that the Recipient can demonstrate:
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;
(b) was lawfully in the Recipient's possession without a duty of confidentiality before disclosure;
(c) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or
(d) is rightfully obtained from a third party who is free to disclose it.

## 3. Obligations of the Recipient
The Recipient will:
(a) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) hold it in strict confidence and protect it with at least the degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, and no less than reasonable care;
(c) not disclose it to any third party except to its directors, employees, professional advisers, and approved subcontractors who need to know it for the Purpose and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than this Agreement (the Recipient remains responsible for their compliance);
(d) not copy or reproduce it except as necessary for the Purpose; and
(e) not reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any disclosed materials except as permitted by mandatory law.

## 4. Compelled disclosure
If the Recipient is required by law, regulation, or a competent authority or court to disclose Confidential Information, it may do so to the extent required, provided that — where legally permitted — it gives the Discloser prompt prior written notice and reasonable assistance to seek protective measures, and discloses only the minimum required.

## 5. No license; no warranty
No license or other right in any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or other intellectual property is granted under this Agreement, whether by implication, estoppel, or otherwise. All Confidential Information remains the property of the Discloser. Confidential Information is provided "as is"; no Party warrants its accuracy or completeness, and no Party is obliged to disclose any particular information or to proceed with the Purpose.

## 6. Term and survival
This Agreement takes effect on the Effective Date and remains in force for `[e.g. 2]` years, unless superseded by a definitive agreement between the Parties. The confidentiality obligations survive termination or expiry for a further `[e.g. 3]` years. For information qualifying as a trade secret under the German Trade Secrets Act (*Gesetz zum Schutz von Geschäftsgeheimnissen, GeschGehG*), the obligations continue for as long as the information remains a trade secret.

## 7. Return or destruction
Upon the Discloser's written request, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Recipient will promptly return or, at the Discloser's option, securely destroy all Confidential Information and copies (including in electronic form) and confirm such destruction in text form. The Recipient may retain (a) one archival copy required by mandatory law or bona fide internal compliance policies, and (b) information in routine, non-targeted electronic backups; any retained information remains subject to this Agreement until deleted.

## 8. Data protection
Where Confidential Information includes personal data, each Party will process it only as necessary for the Purpose and in compliance with the GDPR (DSGVO) and the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG). If processing on behalf of the other Party is intended, the Parties will conclude a separate Data Processing Agreement (*Auftragsverarbeitungsvertrag*) under Art. 28 GDPR.

## 9. Remedies
The Parties acknowledge that a breach may cause harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate. The Discloser is therefore entitled to seek injunctive relief (*einstweilige Verfügung*) and other equitable remedies, in addition to any other remedies available at law, without prejudice to claims under the GeschGehG.

## 10. No partnership; assignment
This Agreement does not create any partnership, joint venture, agency, or obligation to enter into any further agreement. Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent.

## 11. Governing law and jurisdiction
This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Federal Republic of Germany**, excluding its conflict-of-laws rules and the CISG. To the extent permitted by law, the exclusive place of jurisdiction for all disputes is `[CITY]` (Lean-AI's registered seat), where the Counterparty is a merchant, a legal entity under public law, or a special fund under public law.

## 12. Miscellaneous
Amendments must be in text form (*Textform*, § 126b BGB), including any change to this form requirement. If any provision is invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect and the invalid provision is replaced by one closest to its economic intent. This Agreement is the entire understanding of the Parties on its subject matter. Notices may be sent in text form to the addresses above, or to Lean-AI at **hello@lean-ai.com**.
