## 1. Subject matter and duration (Gegenstand und Dauer)

1.1 The subject matter, nature and purpose of the processing, the categories of data subjects and types of personal data are set out in **Annex 1**.

1.2 The duration of the processing corresponds to the term of the Main Agreement and ends with its termination, subject to the deletion/return obligations in Section 11.

## 2. Scope and instructions (Weisungsgebundenheit)

2.1 Lean-AI processes personal data exclusively on the documented instructions of the Client (Art. 28(3)(a) GDPR), including with regard to transfers to third countries, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law; in that case Lean-AI shall inform the Client of that legal requirement before processing, unless prohibited by law.

2.2 The Main Agreement, this AVV and its annexes constitute the initial documented instructions. Further instructions must be given in text form (Textform) by the Client's authorised contacts named in **Annex 1**.

2.3 Lean-AI shall inform the Client without undue delay if it considers that an instruction infringes the GDPR or other data protection provisions (Art. 28(3) GDPR). Lean-AI may suspend the affected processing until the Client confirms or amends the instruction.

## 3. Processor obligations (Pflichten des Auftragsverarbeiters)

Lean-AI shall:

- (a) process personal data only as set out in Section 2;
- (b) ensure that persons authorised to process the data have committed to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory duty of confidentiality (Art. 28(3)(b), Art. 29, Art. 32(4) GDPR);
- (c) implement and maintain the technical and organisational measures in **Annex 2** (Art. 32 GDPR);
- (d) comply with the conditions for engaging sub-processors in Section 5;
- (e) assist the Client as set out in Sections 6 and 7;
- (f) make available to the Client all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Art. 28 GDPR and allow for and contribute to audits (Section 9);
- (g) appoint a data protection officer where legally required, and provide a contact for data-protection matters: `[CONTACT]`;
- (h) maintain a record of processing activities carried out on behalf of the Client (Art. 30(2) GDPR).

## 4. Controller obligations (Pflichten des Verantwortlichen)

The Client is responsible for the lawfulness of the processing and for the rights of the data subjects, in particular for having a valid legal basis. The Client warrants that it is entitled to transfer the personal data to Lean-AI for the agreed processing.

## 5. Sub-processors (Unterauftragsverarbeiter)

5.1 The Client grants Lean-AI **general written authorisation** to engage sub-processors. Lean-AI shall inform the Client of any intended addition or replacement of a sub-processor at least `[14]` days in advance, giving the Client the opportunity to object on reasonable data-protection grounds. If the Client objects in text form and the parties cannot resolve it, the Client may terminate the affected services.

5.2 Lean-AI shall impose on each sub-processor, by contract, data protection obligations equivalent to those in this AVV (Art. 28(4) GDPR), and remains fully liable to the Client for the sub-processor's performance.

5.3 The sub-processors currently approved — **including AI/LLM and cloud providers** — are listed in **Annex 3**.

## 6. Assistance with data-subject rights (Unterstützung Betroffenenrechte)

Taking into account the nature of the processing, Lean-AI shall assist the Client by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, in fulfilling its obligation to respond to requests to exercise data-subject rights under Arts. 12–23 GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection). Lean-AI shall forward any request received directly from a data subject to the Client without undue delay and shall not respond itself unless instructed.

## 7. Assistance with security, breaches and impact assessments

7.1 Lean-AI shall assist the Client, taking into account the nature of processing and information available, in ensuring compliance with Arts. 32–36 GDPR (security of processing, breach notification, data protection impact assessment / DPIA and prior consultation).

7.2 **Breach notification.** Lean-AI shall notify the Client without undue delay, and in any event within `[48]` hours, after becoming aware of a personal data breach (Verletzung des Schutzes personenbezogener Daten) affecting the Client's data. The notification shall include, as far as known, the nature of the breach, categories and approximate number of data subjects and records concerned, likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed (Art. 33(2) GDPR). Lean-AI shall support the Client's notifications to the supervisory authority and data subjects.

## 8. Technical and organisational measures (Technische und organisatorische Maßnahmen / TOMs)

Lean-AI implements appropriate TOMs under Art. 32 GDPR as detailed in **Annex 2**, reviewed regularly and updated to maintain an appropriate level of protection in line with the state of the art.

## 9. Audits (Kontrollrechte)

9.1 Lean-AI shall make available to the Client information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits, including inspections, conducted by the Client or an auditor mandated by it (Art. 28(3)(h) GDPR).

9.2 Audits shall be announced at least `[14]` days in advance, take place during business hours, not unreasonably disrupt operations, and respect Lean-AI's confidentiality and third-party obligations. Lean-AI may first satisfy audit requests by providing up-to-date certifications, reports or self-disclosures (e.g. ISO 27001, SOC 2, where available). On-site audits are limited to `[once per year]` unless triggered by a specific incident.

## 10. International transfers (Drittlandtransfer)

Processing takes place within the EU/EEA unless **Annex 3** states otherwise. Any transfer to a third country requires a valid transfer mechanism under Chapter V GDPR (e.g. adequacy decision, EU Standard Contractual Clauses with supplementary measures) and the Client's documented instruction.

## 11. Deletion and return (Löschung und Rückgabe)

At the Client's choice, Lean-AI shall, upon termination of the processing services, delete or return all personal data processed on the Client's behalf and delete existing copies, unless Union or Member State law requires storage (Art. 28(3)(g) GDPR). Lean-AI shall confirm deletion in text form on request. Routine deletion timeframes are set out in **Annex 1**.

## 12. Liability (Haftung)

Liability between the parties is governed by the GDPR (in particular Art. 82) and the limitations agreed in the Main Agreement, to the extent legally permissible. Liability in the external relationship to data subjects and authorities follows the GDPR.

## 13. Final provisions

Amendments require text form. Should individual provisions be invalid, the remainder of this AVV is unaffected. This AVV is governed by German law.

## Annex 2 — Technical and organisational measures (TOMs)

- **Access control (Zutritts-/Zugangskontrolle):** locked/controlled workspaces; unique user accounts; strong passwords; multi-factor authentication on all relevant systems; auto-lock.
- **Authorisation control (Zugriffskontrolle):** role-based, least-privilege access; access reviews; segregation of client environments/tenants.
- **Encryption (Verschlüsselung):** TLS in transit; encryption at rest for laptops and storage (full-disk / provider-managed); encrypted backups.
- **Pseudonymisation / minimisation:** redact or omit personal data from prompts and test data where feasible; use sample/synthetic data in development.
- **Transmission control (Weitergabekontrolle):** secure file transfer; no personal data over unencrypted channels; controlled use of AI/LLM endpoints per the AI Data-Handling Policy.
- **Input/logging control (Eingabekontrolle):** audit logs for relevant access and changes; versioning.
- **Availability & resilience (Verfügbarkeitskontrolle):** regular backups; restore testing; reputable hosting with redundancy.
- **Separation control (Trennungskontrolle):** logical separation of client data; no commingling.
- **Confidentiality:** confidentiality commitments for all personnel and sub-processors; secrets stored in a secrets manager, never in code or prompts.
- **Processes:** incident response plan; periodic review and updating of TOMs; vendor due diligence.
