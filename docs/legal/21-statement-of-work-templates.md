## SOW Template A — The Diagnostic

*Map your operations, find the constraint, and leave you with a costed, prioritised build plan.*

**SOW No.:** `[NUMBER]` · **Date:** `[DATE]` · **Client:** `[CLIENT NAME]` · **MSA dated:** `[MSA DATE]`

### A.1 Engagement summary
Lean-AI will conduct a focused diagnostic of `[TARGET AREA / PROCESS, e.g. order-to-cash, customer onboarding]`. We **Map** the current operation, surface waste and the binding constraint, and deliver a prioritised, costed plan for what to build next. This is the first move of the Lean-AI arc (Map → Simplify → Build → Hand over).

### A.2 Objectives
- Produce a clear, end-to-end map of the target process and its supporting tools and data.
- Identify the constraint, the largest sources of waste, and the highest-leverage automation opportunities.
- Deliver a ranked, costed roadmap the Client can act on — with or without Lean-AI.

### A.3 In-scope deliverables
1. **Operations map** of the target process (current state), including handoffs, tools, owners, and data flows.
2. **Findings & constraint analysis** — waste, bottlenecks, risk and compliance flags, and quantified opportunity sizing where data allows.
3. **Prioritised build plan** — a ranked backlog of automation/improvement candidates with estimated effort, expected impact, and dependencies.
4. **Readout session** (`[60–90]` minutes) presenting findings and the recommended next move.

### A.4 Explicitly out of scope
- Building, deploying, or operating any system or automation (covered by an Automation Sprint).
- Procurement, licensing, or configuration of third-party software.
- Re-mapping processes outside the agreed target area.
- Change management or staff training.

### A.5 Timeline / milestones
| Milestone | Target |
|---|---|
| Kickoff & data/access provided | `[DATE]` |
| Mapping & interviews complete | `[DATE]` |
| Draft findings shared | `[DATE]` |
| Final readout & build plan delivered | `[DATE]` |

Typical duration: **2–3 weeks** from kickoff, subject to timely Client cooperation (A.8).

### A.6 Fees & payment schedule
- **Fee:** from **€4,500** net (this engagement: **€`[AMOUNT]` net**) plus VAT.
- **Schedule:** **100% invoiced upfront** on signature of this SOW; work begins on receipt of payment.
- **Expenses:** none unless pre-approved in writing per MSA Section 5.

### A.7 Acceptance criteria
The Diagnostic is accepted when the operations map, findings, and prioritised build plan have been delivered and the readout session has been held (or offered and declined). Acceptance follows MSA Section 6, including deemed acceptance after the acceptance period.

### A.8 Client responsibilities & assumptions
- Provide a named point of contact and timely access to relevant people, tools, data, and documentation.
- Make `[N]` stakeholders available for interviews of up to `[DURATION]` each.
- Information provided is assumed accurate and complete; findings depend on it.

### A.9 Change requests
Material changes to scope, target area, or timeline are agreed in text form via a change request signed by both Parties, with any fee/schedule impact stated before work proceeds.

### A.10 Success metrics
- A constraint identified and agreed with the Client.
- A ranked build plan with at least `[N]` actionable, costed opportunities.
- `[OPTIONAL: identified annualised opportunity of €[AMOUNT] / [N] hours per week.]`

## SOW Template B — Automation Sprint

*Build, test, and hand over a working automated system for one defined process.*

**SOW No.:** `[NUMBER]` · **Date:** `[DATE]` · **Client:** `[CLIENT NAME]` · **MSA dated:** `[MSA DATE]`

### B.1 Engagement summary
Lean-AI will **Simplify** and **Build** an automated system for `[PROCESS NAME]`, then **Hand over** a working, documented, measured solution the Client's team owns. Scope is fixed; price is fixed.

### B.2 Objectives
- Remove unnecessary steps from the target process before automating it.
- Build and deploy a reliable automated workflow / AI-agent system for the defined process.
- Hand over a system the Client can run independently, with documentation and monitoring.

### B.3 In-scope deliverables
1. **Streamlined process design** (target state) for `[PROCESS NAME]`.
2. **Working automated system** covering: `[TRIGGER / INPUTS]` → `[STEPS / AI-AGENT ACTIONS]` → `[OUTPUTS / SYSTEMS UPDATED]`, integrated with `[SYSTEMS, e.g. CRM / inbox / ERP / spreadsheet]`.
3. **Testing & validation** against the acceptance criteria in B.7, including a defined set of test cases.
4. **Operating documentation & runbook** — how the system works, how to monitor it, and how to handle exceptions.
5. **Handover & enablement session** (`[60–120]` minutes) with the responsible team.
6. **Monitoring / measurement setup** for the agreed success metrics (B.10).

### B.4 Explicitly out of scope
- Processes other than `[PROCESS NAME]`.
- Ongoing operation, monitoring, or maintenance after handover (covered by an Operating Partner retainer).
- Custom development of third-party platforms or unsupported integrations.
- Migration of historical data beyond what is needed to run the new system.
- Procurement of, or payment for, third-party licences, subscriptions, or AI/API usage fees (Client's own accounts; see B.8).

### B.5 Timeline / milestones
| Milestone | Target |
|---|---|
| Kickoff, access & accounts provisioned | `[DATE]` |
| Streamlined design signed off | `[DATE]` |
| Build complete / system in test | `[DATE]` |
| Acceptance testing & fixes | `[DATE]` |
| Handover session & sign-off | `[DATE]` |

Typical duration: **4–6 weeks** from kickoff, subject to timely Client cooperation (B.8).

### B.6 Fees & payment schedule
- **Fee:** from **€12,000** net (this engagement: **€`[AMOUNT]` net**) plus VAT.
- **Schedule:** **50% invoiced upfront** on signature of this SOW; **50% invoiced on acceptance** under B.7 (MSA Section 6). Both invoices net 14 days.
- **Expenses / third-party costs:** the Client bears its own software, subscription, and AI/API usage costs on its own accounts; any costs Lean-AI incurs on the Client's behalf are pre-approved in writing per MSA Section 5.

### B.7 Acceptance criteria
The system is accepted when it:
- processes the agreed test cases end-to-end with at least `[ACCURACY/SUCCESS THRESHOLD, e.g. 95%]` success on defined inputs;
- correctly handles the defined exception/edge cases listed in `[APPENDIX]`;
- is documented per the runbook deliverable; and
- has passed the handover session.

Acceptance follows MSA Section 6 (including deemed acceptance and the treatment of minor defects).

### B.8 Client responsibilities & assumptions
- Provide accounts, API keys, licences, and access to all systems the automation must touch, by `[DATE]`.
- Name a process owner empowered to make decisions and approve the target design.
- Provide representative sample data and confirm the exception cases to be handled.
- Ensure third-party platforms permit the intended automation under their terms.
- Assumption: the target process is stable enough to automate; major process redefinition mid-sprint is a change request.

### B.9 Change requests
Changes to scope, integrations, or acceptance criteria are handled by a written change request signed by both Parties. Lean-AI will state the impact on fee and timeline before proceeding. Out-of-scope requests may be quoted as a new SOW.

### B.10 Success metrics
- Time per transaction reduced from `[BASELINE]` to `[TARGET]`.
- `[N]` manual hours per `[week/month]` removed.
- Error/rework rate reduced from `[BASELINE]` to `[TARGET]`.
- System live and owned by the Client's team at handover.

## SOW Template C — Operating Partner (retainer)

*Run, measure, and keep improving the systems we built — month after month.*

**SOW No.:** `[NUMBER]` · **Date:** `[DATE]` · **Client:** `[CLIENT NAME]` · **MSA dated:** `[MSA DATE]`

### C.1 Engagement summary
Lean-AI acts as the Client's ongoing operating partner for `[SYSTEMS / PROCESS AREA]`: monitoring the systems, resolving issues, measuring performance, and shipping a steady stream of improvements. This is the "keep the keys working" phase of the arc.

### C.2 Objectives
- Keep the in-scope systems running reliably and measured.
- Continuously remove waste and add high-leverage improvements each month.
- Give the Client clear visibility through regular reporting and a standing working rhythm.

### C.3 In-scope deliverables (per month)
1. **Monitoring & issue resolution** for the in-scope systems, within the agreed response targets.
2. **Continuous improvement** — an agreed allocation of effort toward new automations, refinements, and waste removal, prioritised with the Client.
3. **Monthly performance report** against the success metrics (C.10).
4. **Standing working session** (`[cadence, e.g. bi-weekly]`) to review priorities and progress.
5. **Maintained documentation** so the Client retains ownership and understanding.

### C.4 Explicitly out of scope
- Large new builds beyond the agreed monthly allocation (handled as a separate Automation Sprint SOW).
- 24/7 on-call or guaranteed real-time response unless expressly agreed in C.7.
- Systems or processes not listed in the in-scope schedule.
- Third-party licence and usage costs (Client's own accounts).

### C.5 In-scope systems
`[LIST THE SYSTEMS / PROCESSES COVERED]`

### C.6 Term, minimum commitment & notice
- **Minimum commitment:** one calendar quarter, per MSA Section 12.3.
- **After the minimum term:** continues monthly until terminated; either Party may terminate at the end of a calendar month on `[30]` days' written notice.

### C.7 Service levels (optional)
- Acknowledge issues within `[e.g. 1 business day]`; begin work on critical issues within `[e.g. 4 business hours]` during `[business hours, time zone]`.
- Monthly improvement allocation: approximately `[N]` hours / `[N]` initiatives, non-cumulative unless agreed.

### C.8 Fees & payment schedule
- **Fee:** from **€3,500 per month** net (this engagement: **€`[AMOUNT]`/month net**) plus VAT.
- **Schedule:** invoiced **monthly in advance**; net 14 days. The first quarter's commitment is binding per C.6.
- **Expenses / third-party costs:** as MSA Section 5; Client bears its own platform/AI usage costs.

### C.9 Client responsibilities & assumptions
- Maintain Lean-AI's access to the in-scope systems and accounts throughout the term.
- Name a single owner for prioritisation decisions.
- Keep its third-party subscriptions and licences active and paid.

### C.10 Success metrics
- Uptime / reliability of in-scope systems at or above `[TARGET]`.
- `[N]` improvements shipped per quarter.
- Sustained reduction in manual effort and error rate vs. the Diagnostic/Sprint baseline.
- Reported monthly; reviewed quarterly with the Client.

### C.11 Change requests
