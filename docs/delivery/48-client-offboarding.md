# Client Offboarding & Engagement Close-Out

*End the engagement as cleanly as we started it: full acceptance, knowledge transferred, keys handed over, and the relationship left open for what comes next.*

How an engagement ends shapes whether it renews, expands, or becomes a case study. This checklist closes out a Diagnostic, a Sprint, or a retainer. The goal is simple: the client owns a working system and the knowledge to run it, Lean-AI retains nothing it shouldn't, and the door to the next engagement is held open honestly.

```
Client:            __________________________________
Engagement:        Diagnostic / Sprint / Operating Partner
Close-out date:    __________
Owner accepting:   __________________________________
```

---

## 1. Final acceptance

- [ ] All deliverables in the SOW delivered and confirmed.
- [ ] For a Sprint: acceptance sign-off complete (see `47-qa-and-handover-checklist.md`).
- [ ] **Measured outcome vs. baseline** documented and shared (hours/€/cycle time/error rate).
- [ ] Any open items either closed or explicitly logged with an agreed owner.
- [ ] Final invoice / balance settled.

---

## 2. Knowledge transfer

- [ ] SOP, runbook, and as-built design handed over and living in the client's workspace.
- [ ] Walkthrough videos delivered.
- [ ] Named internal owner confirmed they can run, monitor, and recover the system unaided.
- [ ] A final Q&A pass held to clear any remaining questions.
- [ ] Reusable components folded into Lean-AI's **template library** (the compounding asset).

---

## 3. Credentials & access handover

- [ ] All accounts, keys, and integrations confirmed on the **client's tenancy** — their data, their keys.
- [ ] Secrets re-issued/rotated so no Lean-AI-held credential retains access.
- [ ] Lean-AI's standing access **revoked** (or reduced to the agreed support level only).
- [ ] Tooling billing confirmed on the client's accounts.
- [ ] **Client data held by Lean-AI deleted** per the data-protection terms; deletion confirmed in writing.
- [ ] Access/deletion completion noted: ______________  Date: __________

---

## 4. Post-engagement support options

State the options plainly; let the client choose.

| Option | What it covers | When it fits |
|---|---|---|
| **30-day window** (Sprint only) | Defects + tuning in original scope | Immediately after a Sprint |
| **Operating Partner** (€3,500/mo) | Rolling roadmap, monthly increment, continuous improvement | Ongoing improvement / more on the roadmap |
| **New Sprint** (€12,000) | A new end-to-end process | A distinct next process to automate |
| **Ad-hoc / none** | No standing commitment | System is steady-state and self-run |

- [ ] Support option chosen and documented: __________

---

## 5. Retainer / expansion conversation

Have this deliberately, not as an afterthought — but only recommend what's genuinely valuable (honesty is the brand).

- [ ] Recap the measured outcome and what's still on the roadmap.
- [ ] If there is a real body of next work: propose **Operating Partner** (to execute the roadmap steadily) or a **new Sprint** (for a distinct next process).
- [ ] Name the next available start date truthfully (WIP limit: one active build at a time).
- [ ] If the system is steady-state and there's no real next value: say so. Offer to stay reachable rather than sell a low-value retainer.
- [ ] Outcome logged in the pipeline: __________

---

## 6. Testimonial & case-study request

Ask while the result is fresh and the client is pleased.

- [ ] Request a short **testimonial** (2–3 sentences on the outcome).
- [ ] Request permission for an **anonymised or named case study** — what we built, the measured result.
- [ ] Confirm what may be disclosed (numbers, name, logo) and what must stay confidential.
- [ ] If a public case study is approved, draft it (AI-assisted) and get sign-off before publishing.
- [ ] Add the result to Lean-AI's proof pillars / portfolio.

---

## 7. Relationship continuity

- [ ] Schedule a **check-in** in 30/60/90 days as appropriate (light-touch, async).
- [ ] Confirm the founder remains the point of contact for future needs.
- [ ] Note any referral opportunity the client mentioned.
- [ ] Send a short, personal close-out note from the founder — plain, warm, no boilerplate.

---

## 8. Close-out gate

The engagement is closed only when **all** are true:

- [ ] Final acceptance and payment complete.
- [ ] Knowledge and documentation transferred.
- [ ] Credentials handed over; Lean-AI access revoked; client data deleted and confirmed.
- [ ] Support path chosen.
- [ ] Renewal/expansion conversation held and logged.
- [ ] Testimonial / case-study requested.
- [ ] Continuity check-in scheduled.
