# SOP — Operating Partner (Retainer)

*The monthly cadence that keeps a client's operating system improving — one build increment a month, ~15 hours, on a quarterly rhythm.*

This is the standard operating procedure for the **Operating Partner** retainer: the rolling relationship that follows a Sprint (or a Diagnostic-led roadmap). Each month delivers a roadmap, one shipped build increment, a continuous-improvement pass, and an operating review. The client gets a partner who keeps the system getting better; Lean-AI gets predictable revenue inside the WIP limit.

**Offer facts (do not change):** Operating Partner — €3,500/month net of VAT, quarterly minimum. Includes: rolling roadmap, monthly build increment, continuous improvement, operating reviews.

**WIP rule:** up to ~2 retainers run alongside one active build. A retainer is ~15 hrs/month — protect that ceiling.

---

## 1. Monthly time budget (~15 hrs)

| Activity | Hours |
|---|---|
| Roadmap management + prioritisation | 1.5 |
| Monthly build increment | 9 |
| Continuous-improvement loop (monitor, tune, measure) | 2 |
| Operating review (prep + 45-min call) | 1.5 |
| Reporting (async, AI-drafted) | 1 |
| **Total** | **15** |

Each retainer runs on the same fixed monthly cadence so two can coexist without thrashing.

---

## 2. The monthly cadence

A repeatable four-week loop. Async-first; one live call per month.

### Week 1 — Plan
- [ ] Review the **rolling roadmap**; reprioritise with the sponsor (effort × impact, value first).
- [ ] Pick **the one build increment** for the month and define its done.
- [ ] Confirm any access/data needs.

### Weeks 2–3 — Build
- [ ] Ship the monthly increment: a new automation, an extension, an integration, or a measurable improvement to an existing system.
- [ ] Build against real cases; keep human-in-the-loop gates; instrument it.
- [ ] Reuse and contribute to the template library.
- [ ] Send a short async mid-month update (AI-drafted from the work log, founder-signed).

### Week 4 — Improve, review, report
- [ ] Run the **continuous-improvement loop** (section 4).
- [ ] Hold the **operating review** (section 3).
- [ ] Send the **monthly report** (section 5).
- [ ] Roll unfinished items into next month's roadmap.

---

## 3. Operating review agenda (~45 min, monthly)

1. **Outcomes since last review** (10 min) — what shipped, measured against the system's KPIs.
2. **System health** (5 min) — uptime, exceptions, anything humans had to catch.
3. **This month's increment** (10 min) — what we built, the result, a quick demo.
4. **Continuous improvement** (5 min) — tuning done, waste removed, what improved.
5. **Roadmap & next increment** (10 min) — reprioritise; agree next month's focus.
6. **Risks, blockers, decisions needed** (5 min).

Keep it tight and concrete. Numbers, not narrative.

---

## 4. Continuous-improvement loop

Every month, run a light Lean/Kaizen pass on the live systems:

- [ ] **Monitor** — review dashboards and logs; where are humans still intervening?
- [ ] **Measure** — compare current KPIs to baseline and to last month.
- [ ] **Find waste** — recurring exceptions, manual steps, slow hand-offs, drift.
- [ ] **Tune** — adjust prompts, thresholds, routing; remove a step; raise automation rate.
- [ ] **Re-measure** — confirm the change helped; log the delta.
- [ ] Feed anything larger than a tune into the roadmap as a candidate increment.

## 6. Renewal & expansion motion

The retainer auto-continues past the quarterly minimum unless cancelled. Treat each quarter-end as a deliberate value conversation, not an admin formality.

- [ ] **Quarterly value recap** — assemble the quarter's measured outcomes into one page; lead the review with it.
- [ ] **Renewal** — confirm continuation; the WIP limit makes the slot scarce — say so truthfully.
- [ ] **Expansion** — if the roadmap holds a body of work too big for the monthly increment, scope it as a new **Automation Sprint** (€12,000) and slot it against the next build window.
- [ ] **Right-sizing** — if value has plateaued and the system is steady-state, say so; offer to step down to a lighter cadence or pause rather than bill for low-value months. That honesty protects the relationship and the brand.
- [ ] Log renewal/expansion outcomes in the pipeline.

---

## 7. Definition of done (each retainer month)

- [ ] The monthly build increment shipped and measured.
- [ ] Continuous-improvement pass completed and logged.
- [ ] Operating review held.
- [ ] Monthly report sent.
- [ ] Roadmap updated; next increment agreed.
- [ ] Hours ≤ 15 (if exceeded two months running → renegotiate scope or price).
