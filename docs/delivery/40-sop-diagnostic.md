# SOP — The Diagnostic

*How we deliver a €4,500 value-stream and AI-readiness audit in 2–3 weeks, inside a ~30-hour budget.*

This is the standard operating procedure for delivering **The Diagnostic**. It is the front door to almost every engagement: it produces a value-stream map, an AI-readiness assessment, a prioritised opportunity backlog, a business case, and a 12-month roadmap — and it ends with a clear recommendation to run an Automation Sprint, with the €4,500 fee creditable against it.

**Offer facts (do not change):** The Diagnostic — €4,500 net of VAT, 2–3 weeks, creditable to a Sprint.

---

## 1. Outcomes & deliverable

By the end of the engagement the client has a single branded PDF/Markdown deliverable containing:

- **Executive summary** — what we found, what it costs them today, what we recommend.
- **Current-state value-stream map** — using `44-value-stream-mapping-template.md`.
- **AI-readiness assessment** — scored across seven dimensions, using `45-ai-readiness-assessment.md`.
- **Prioritised opportunity backlog** — effort × impact, with the recommended first Sprint named.
- **Business case** — annual cost of waste today, expected payback of the recommended Sprint.
- **12-month roadmap** — sequenced quarters: Lean simplification, automation increments, capability build.
- **Recommendation & next step** — the Sprint scope and start date.

> The deliverable must *feel* like the website: serif headers, gold accent, lots of whitespace, plain confident prose.

## 2. Roles

| Role | Who | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| Lead | Founder | Diagnosis, interviews, synthesis, readout, recommendation. Owns the relationship. |

| Client sponsor | Client exec | Unblocks access, names process owners, attends kickoff + readout. |

## 4. Day-by-day plan (across 2–3 weeks, async-first)

### Week 1 — Map the ground truth

**Day 1 — Kickoff (1 live call, ~45 min)**
- [ ] Confirm the process(es) in scope and the single business question we are answering.
- [ ] Confirm sponsor + the 3–5 process owners we will interview.
- [ ] Walk through the timeline, the async working style, and what we need from them.
- [ ] Issue the **intake questionnaire** and the **document/access request** (see `43-client-onboarding-checklist.md`).
- *AI assist:* the **intake agent** pre-fills a draft assessment from the questionnaire and any documents the moment they land.

**Days 2–3 — Intake review (async)**
- [ ] Read the returned questionnaire + documents; have the intake agent structure them.
- [ ] List open questions and the specific steps you need to see.
- [ ] Book the interview/shadowing slots (15–30 min each, recorded with consent).

**Days 4–5 — Interviews & shadowing**
- [ ] Interview each process owner: walk the process end-to-end, capture step time, wait time, rework, hand-offs, pain.
- [ ] Where possible, watch the work happen (screen-share or recorded walkthrough) rather than only hearing it described.
- [ ] Capture systems touched, data sources, and where decisions need a human.
- *AI assist:* the **process-map / SOP generator** turns interview transcripts into a first-draft value-stream map and step list.

### Week 2 — Analyse and design

**Days 6–7 — Value-stream map**
- [ ] Complete the current-state map in `44-value-stream-mapping-template.md`: steps, time, wait, rework, the 8 wastes.
- [ ] Quantify the cost of the current state in **hours/year and €/year**.
- [ ] Send the draft map to process owners for a 24-hour async validation pass. **Lean before automation** — fix the process on paper first.
- [ ] Sketch the **target state**: what disappears, what simplifies, what is automated.

**Days 8–9 — AI-readiness assessment**
- [ ] Score the seven dimensions in `45-ai-readiness-assessment.md` (data, tooling, process maturity, skills, security/compliance, leadership buy-in, change readiness).
- [ ] Note constraints that shape any future build — especially **EU data-protection and human-in-the-loop** requirements.
- *AI assist:* draft scoring rationale from intake + interview evidence; founder sets final scores.

**Days 10–11 — Backlog, business case, roadmap**
- [ ] Build the **prioritised opportunity backlog**: each opportunity scored effort × impact, with expected hours/€ saved.
- [ ] Write the **business case** for the top opportunity: cost of waste today vs. Sprint price (€12,000) and expected payback period.
- [ ] Sequence the **12-month roadmap** by quarter (simplify → first Sprint → increments → capability).
- [ ] Name the **recommended first Sprint** explicitly.

### Week 3 (or end of week 2) — Deliver and convert

**Day 12 — Assemble the deliverable**
- [ ] Compose the branded document; founder edits every AI-drafted section for accuracy and voice.
- [ ] Sanity-check every number. Remove anything you cannot defend in the readout.

**Day 13 — Readout (1 live call, ~60 min) + send**
- [ ] Present: what we found → what it costs → what we recommend → the roadmap.
- [ ] Send the written deliverable within 24 hours of the call (never present without leaving the document).

---

## 5. The readout (agenda)

1. **The question we set out to answer** (30 sec) — recap scope.
2. **Current state** (10 min) — the map, the waste, the cost in €/year. Be concrete.
3. **AI-readiness** (5 min) — the score, the honest gaps, the constraints (data protection, human-in-the-loop).
4. **The opportunity backlog** (10 min) — top 3, effort × impact.
5. **Recommendation** (10 min) — the first Sprint, the business case, the payback.
6. **Roadmap** (5 min) — the 12-month sequence.
7. **Next step** (10 min) — the conversion motion below.

> Honesty rule: if the right answer is "fix the process, you don't need automation yet," say it. That candor is the marketing.

---

## 6. Conversion-to-Sprint motion

- [ ] In the readout, end on the named first Sprint, its scope, its payback, and the **next available start date** (respect the WIP limit — one active build at a time).
- [ ] State plainly: **the €4,500 Diagnostic fee is credited against the €12,000 Sprint** if they proceed within 30 days.
- [ ] Send the Sprint SOW (fixed scope, fixed price) within 24 hours of the readout, with the credit applied on the first line.
- [ ] If they are not ready to Sprint, offer the **Operating Partner** retainer as the path to execute the roadmap over time, or set a follow-up date.
- [ ] Log the outcome in the pipeline; if won, begin `43-client-onboarding-checklist.md`. Never start build work before the deposit clears.

---

## 7. Definition of done (Diagnostic)

- [ ] Current-state value-stream map validated by process owners.
- [ ] Cost of waste quantified in hours/year and €/year.
- [ ] AI-readiness scored across all seven dimensions with rationale.
- [ ] Prioritised backlog with a named, costed first Sprint.
- [ ] Business case with payback period.
- [ ] 12-month roadmap.
- [ ] Readout delivered; written deliverable sent within 24 hours.
- [ ] Conversion motion executed (SOW sent or follow-up booked).
- [ ] Engagement closed in pipeline; founder hours logged for the dogfooding benchmark.
