# Lean-AI — Brand & Positioning Guide

*The words, the look, and the rules that make Lean-AI feel premium.*

---

## 1. Positioning statement

> **For** ambitious small and mid-sized companies that know AI matters but lack the team to execute it, **Lean-AI** is the operating partner that designs and *builds* AI-native operating systems — removing waste with Lean, automating the rest with AI agents, and handing your team a measured system they own. **Unlike** consultancies that advise and leave, **Lean-AI** ships running systems with fixed scope, fixed price, and proven outcomes.

**Category we play in:** *operations studio / transformation partner* — not "consulting," not "agency."

---

## 2. The name

**Lean-AI** says exactly what we do: we fuse **Lean** — the operational-excellence discipline of removing waste — with **AI** that does the work. The name is descriptive and confident: it tells an SME leader in two words that this is operational rigor plus modern automation, not hype. It also mirrors the founder's own credentials (Lean Six Sigma Black Belt) and pairs naturally with the tagline that carries the promise: *the operating partner for the AI era.* Pronounce it simply: *"lean A-I."*

Wordmark: set in a refined serif, letter-spaced, uppercase — **LEAN-AI** — keeping the hyphen. The mark is a single ascending stroke (the Lean improvement curve) resolving into a clean node — waste removed, system running.

Domain & handles: primary **lean-ai.com**; secure **lean-ai.de** (DACH trust) and **lean-ai.ai** (on-brand), redirecting to .com. Match the LinkedIn and social handles to keep the brand consistent.

---

## 3. One-liners & messaging ladder

- **Tagline:** *The operating partner for the AI era.*
- **One-liner:** *We build the AI-native operating systems behind great companies.*
- **Elevator (30 sec):** *Lean-AI helps ambitious SMEs turn AI from hype into operations that actually run. We remove the waste with Lean, automate the rest with AI agents, and hand your team a system they own — fixed scope, fixed price, measured outcomes. We don't sell advice; we build the system, prove it works, and hand you the keys.*

**Proof pillars** (always back claims with these):
1. **Built, not advised** — a running system, not a deck.
2. **Lean before automation** — we don't automate broken processes.
3. **Adoption is the product** — servant leadership; your team owns it.
4. **Measured outcomes** — instrumented, payback-oriented.

---

## 4. Voice & tone

- **Confident, not loud.** Premium brands state; they don't shout. Few adjectives, strong nouns and verbs.
- **Plain and concrete.** Real numbers, real processes, real timelines. Avoid buzzword soup.
- **Honest.** We tell clients when we *can't* help. That candor is the marketing.
- **Human.** Servant leadership shows in the language: we serve teams, we hand over ownership.

**Use:** transformation partner · operating partner · operating system · outcomes, not hours · done-with-you, then done-for-you · running system · the keys.
**Avoid:** consultant · freelancer · cheap · fast · "automations" · "Zaps" · "let's hop on a call" · synergy/leverage/disrupt as filler.

## 5. Visual identity

**Palette**
| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#0E1116` | Primary dark; hero, dark sections, text. |
| Ink-2 | `#161B22` | Panels, cards on dark. |
| Bone | `#F7F4ED` | Warm off-white; light backgrounds. |
| Paper | `#FBFAF6` | Page background. |
| Gold | `#B68A3E` | Restrained accent — one accent, used sparingly. |
| Gold-soft | `#C8A35B` | Accent on dark backgrounds. |

The restraint *is* the premium: lots of whitespace, one accent colour, no gradients-as-decoration beyond subtle ambient light.

**Typography**
- Display: **Fraunces** (serif) — editorial, warm, high-end.
- Body/UI: **Inter** (sans) — clean, neutral, legible.

**Principles**
- Generous whitespace; let headlines breathe.
- Serif for emotion (headlines, pull quotes), sans for clarity (body, UI).
- Motion is subtle: gentle reveal-on-scroll only. Never bouncy.

- **LinkedIn** — primary authority channel; same voice, same restraint.
- **Proposals & deliverables** — branded, serif headers, gold accent, lots of white space. The deliverable should *feel* like the website.

---

## 7. Do / Don't quick reference

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with outcomes and systems | Lead with tools or hours |
| Show one strong accent colour | Use rainbow palettes or stock-photo gradients |
| Write short, concrete, honest | Write buzzword-dense hype |
| Say "we build" | Say "we advise / we consult" |
| Use scarcity truthfully (WIP limit) | Manufacture fake urgency |
| Be your own case study | Over-claim client results you can't show |
