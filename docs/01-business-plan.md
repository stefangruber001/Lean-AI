*The operating partner for the AI era.*

**Version 1.0 · Confidential · Prepared for the founder**

---

## 0. How to read this plan

This is a working founder's plan, not an investor pitch. It is built around three hard constraints you set:

1. **This runs alongside a full-time job** — budget ~**10–15 hours/week**.
2. **Premium positioning** — a transformation partner that *builds*, not a commodity consultancy that *advises*.
3. **First-time-right execution** — every recommendation here is designed to be acted on, not debated.

Everything that follows is shaped by those three constraints. When a choice trades growth speed for time-protection, this plan chooses time-protection — because a side business that burns out the founder is worth nothing.

## 1. Executive summary

**Lean-AI** is a premium, AI-native operations studio that designs and deploys working operating systems for ambitious small and mid-sized companies (SMEs). We combine four disciplines into one method:

- **AI agents** to do repetitive, rules-based, data-heavy work reliably and observably;
- **Lean methodology** to remove waste *before* automating, so we never "automate a broken process";
- **Servant leadership** to drive real adoption and hand ownership back to the client's team;
- **Operational excellence** to instrument every process so improvement compounds.

We do not sell decks. Every engagement ends with a running system the client owns. The business is deliberately **productized** — three fixed-scope, fixed-price offers — so that delivery is time-bounded and the founder's hours are protected.

**The wedge:** the German *Mittelstand* and European SMEs have high intent but low execution on AI. Mid-sized adoption sits near ~28% and small-firm adoption near ~17% (vs. ~48% for large enterprises), and ~43% of mid-sized firms still have *no concrete AI plan*. They lack in-house skill, trust in AI with their data, and clarity amid tool overchoice. Lean-AI converts that intent into outcomes.

**The model:** a paid **Diagnostic** (the front door) → a fixed-scope **Automation Sprint** (the build) → an optional **Operating Partner** retainer (the compounding engine). Value-based pricing, not hours. Few high-value clients, not many cheap ones.

**Year-1 ambition (conservative base case):** ~€90–100k revenue at 75%+ contribution margin, delivered inside the founder's part-time hour budget, with the operating discipline in place to scale in Year 2.

## 2. The problem we solve

SMEs know AI matters. What they lack is a partner who turns the hype into operations that run on Monday morning. Concretely, the recurring pain (validated in market research, see `02-market-research.md`):

- **Skill gap.** ~46% of leaders cite skill gaps as a top AI barrier. SMEs have no internal team to design and build safely.
- **ROI uncertainty.** Only ~1 in 4 AI initiatives deliver the expected ROI. Leaders are burned and cautious.
- **Trust & data security.** Deep distrust of cloud LLMs handling sensitive financial and customer data.
- **Overchoice & complexity.** A crowded vendor market, few tools tailored to SME realities, training too theoretical.
- **Abandoned consultants.** Traditional consultancies diagnose, present, and leave nothing that runs.

The gap a premium partner fills: **assess → simplify → build → train → operate → expand**, owning the outcome and de-risking the ROI. Lean + servant leadership + operational excellence is the differentiator that separates Lean-AI from commodity "automation agencies" selling disconnected Zaps.

## 3. The solution — the Lean-AI Method

A four-move engagement arc that is identical in shape for every client, which is what makes it productizable:

1. **Map** — follow the work, map the value stream end to end, quantify the friction in hours and euros.
2. **Simplify** — strip waste, redesign the flow, define the leaner target state *before* automation.
3. **Build** — deploy AI agents and automations, integrate the client's tools, ship a tested working system.
4. **Hand over** — train the team, document the system, set the metrics, transfer ownership.

This is Lean (map/simplify) + AI engineering (build) + servant leadership and operational excellence (hand over + measure) expressed as a repeatable delivery pattern.

## 4. Offerings (productized, fixed-scope, premium)

| Offer | Price (from) | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Diagnostic** | €4,500 | 2–3 weeks | Paid front door. Value-stream + AI-readiness audit, prioritized opportunity backlog, business case, 12-month roadmap. Fully creditable toward a Sprint. |
| **Automation Sprint** | €12,000 | 4–6 weeks | The core build. One end-to-end process automated, AI agents + integrations deployed, team trained, dashboards live, 30-day post-launch support. |
| **Operating Partner** | €3,500/mo | Quarterly min. | The compounding engine. Rolling roadmap, monthly build cadence, continuous improvement, operating reviews. |

**Why this structure works for a part-time founder:**

- **Fixed scope removes negotiation** and caps hours per engagement.
- **The Diagnostic de-risks the buyer** (small first commitment) *and* the founder (you scope the build with full information, and it's a paid sales process).
- **The retainer turns one-off projects into predictable recurring revenue** — the single most important lever for a sustainable side business.

Pricing is anchored to comparables in the market (a representative hybrid player charges ~$5k assessment + ~$3.5k/mo; premium fractional/operational engagements run €1,200–2,500+/day in DACH). See `03-financial-model.md`.

## 9. Operations & the 10–15 hr/week model (summary)

The business is engineered to fit a part-time founder (full detail in `05-operating-model.md`):

- **WIP limit:** one active build at a time + up to ~2 retainers. A Lean/Kanban constraint that is also a premium scarcity signal.
- **Async-first delivery** to minimize meeting load.
- **AI leverage internally** — intake, process maps, SOPs, proposals, and updates drafted by our own agents (eat our own dog food).
- **First hire = a flat-rate/retainer subcontractor** (candidate: "MB"), taking queue items — *not* an hourly employee. Hire only when a retainer base reliably funds it.

## 10. Legal & setup (Germany/DACH — general info, not legal advice)

- **Get written employer permission** for the side activity before invoicing the first client.
- **Start simple:** Einzelunternehmen (or Freiberufler if the Finanzamt classifies the work as liberal-profession — confirm with a Steuerberater).
- **VAT:** the Kleinunternehmer scheme (2025 rules: prior-year ≤ €25k, current-year forecast ≤ €100k). For B2B clients VAT is reclaimable, so **opting into standard VAT (19% USt) is often neutral to clients and avoids a "small/cheap" signal** — weigh against admin simplicity.
- Confirm legal form, Freiberufler-vs-Gewerbe classification, and VAT election with a German Steuerberater/Rechtsanwalt before launch.

## 12. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Founder time overrun / burnout | Hard WIP limit; async-first; productized fixed scope; raise prices before adding volume. |
| Over-reliance on founder | Templatize and document everything from day one; bring in subcontractor early for delivery overflow. |
| Client data/trust concerns | EU-compliant, privacy-first architecture; clear data boundaries; document data flows; offer on-prem/closed-model options. |
| AI output reliability | Human-in-the-loop on launch; metrics + monitoring; 30-day post-launch support baked into the Sprint. |
| Pricing pressure / commoditization | Stay senior-only, niche-focused, outcome-priced; never compete on hourly rate. |
| Employer conflict | Written permission up front; no clients that compete with employer; deliver outside working hours. |
| Pipeline dependence on founder's network | Build the content/authority engine from week 1 so inbound compounds beyond the warm network. |
