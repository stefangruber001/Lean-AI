# Lean-AI — OKRs & Roadmap

*What "good" looks like each year, and the path to the Year-3 fork.*

> Targets are consistent with `docs/03-financial-model.md` (base case): Year 1 ~€95k / ~87% margin / 2 retainers; Year 2 ~€200k / ~72% / 5 retainers; Year 3 ~€320k / ~66% / 8 retainers. All revenue net of VAT. Everything fits inside 10–15 hrs/week via the WIP limit.

---

## 1. Annual OKRs

### Year 1 — *Prove the model, stay cash-positive, protect the day job*

**O1. Land and deliver the first paying clients without breaking the time budget.**
- KR1: Sign **≥4 Sprints** and **≥6 Diagnostics** (per the model).
- KR2: End the year with **2 active retainers**.
- KR3: Hit **~€95k revenue** at **~87% margin**.
- KR4: Founder hours **stay ≤15/week** every month (health metric).

**O2. Build the compounding asset (templates + internal agents).**
- KR1: Ship the core internal agents: intake, SOP/process-map generator, proposal drafter, client-update agent (see `docs/05-operating-model.md`).
- KR2: Turn **every** client build into a reusable parameterised template.
- KR3: Publish one credible internal-dogfooding figure (*"X% of delivery hours removed by my own agents"*).

**O3. Establish a premium, trusted brand and a warm pipeline.**
- KR1: **Retainer attach rate ≥40%** (Sprint → retainer).
- KR2: Maintain a **booked fit-call pipeline** sufficient to keep the queue full without discounting.
- KR3: One referenceable lighthouse client / case study.

**O4. Set the business on clean legal/financial rails.**
- KR1: Written **employer permission** secured before invoicing client #1.
- KR2: Legal form, VAT posture, and bookkeeping confirmed with a Steuerberater; §14-compliant invoicing live.
- KR3: Tax provisions swept monthly from invoice #1.

### Year 2 — *Add leverage, double revenue, keep margin healthy*

**O1. Scale delivery with a flat-rate subcontractor.** KRs: onboard **1 subcontractor** once retainers fund it; subcontractor handles build/integration/docs/QA; founder still owns relationship + diagnosis.
**O2. Grow recurring revenue.** KRs: **5 active retainers** year-end; **~€200k revenue** at **~72% margin**.
**O3. Mature the asset.** KRs: template reuse measurably lowers hours-per-Sprint; price raised ~10–15% after sustained ≥80% capacity.

### Year 3 — *Reach the fork with options open*

**O1. Operate a senior, recurring-heavy book.** KRs: **8 active retainers**; **~€320k revenue** at **~66% margin**.
**O2. De-risk key-person dependence.** KRs: subcontractor(s) deliver independently from SOPs; documented continuity plan.
**O3. Make the strategic decision deliberately** (see §3).

## 3. Three-year roadmap & the decision point

| Year | Theme | End state |
|---|---|---|
| **Year 1** | Prove & protect | 2 retainers · ~€95k · ~87% margin · solo · asset-light, cash-positive, no debt |
| **Year 2** | Leverage | 5 retainers · ~€200k · ~72% margin · 1 flat-rate subcontractor · template-driven delivery |
| **Year 3** | The fork | 8 retainers · ~€320k · ~66% margin · senior delivery from SOPs · **strategic decision due** |

### Year-3 decision: stay side-business vs. go full-time

At ~€320k and a maturing retainer base, choose deliberately:

1. **Stay a premium side business.** Cap growth, keep margin sky-high, protect the day job. Hold the WIP limit; let price (not volume) absorb demand.
2. **Go full-time and scale.** Convert subcontractor(s) into a **small senior team** (see `docs/people/70-hiring-plan-and-subcontractor-role.md`), revisit legal form (UG/GmbH → Bilanz; see `docs/finance/51-cashflow-and-bookkeeping.md`), and push toward €750k–1M on the same productized model.

**Decision inputs:** retainer stability and churn; subcontractor independence; personal capacity/appetite for full-time; tax/liability picture from the Steuerberater; whether demand can be met on price alone. Make it a scheduled, written review — not a drift.
