# Lean-AI — Cashflow & Bookkeeping

*A 30-minutes-a-month money routine for a part-time, cash-positive studio.*

> **General information, not tax or legal advice.** Categories, percentages, and deadlines below are working defaults for a German solo business. Your obligations depend on your legal form, VAT posture, and income — confirm with a **Steuerberater**.

---

## 1. The monthly money routine (~30 min)

Run on a fixed day each month (e.g. the 1st). Calendar-block it.

1. **Reconcile the bank.** Match every transaction in the business account to an invoice (in) or a categorised cost (out). See `50-invoicing-process.md` §6.
2. **Chase overdue invoices.** Trigger the dunning ladder for anything past net-14.
3. **Sweep tax provisions.** Move the set-aside percentages (see §3) into the tax sub-account.
4. **Categorise costs** into the buckets in §2.
5. **Update the cashflow forecast** (see §5) — 3 months ahead.
6. **Export for the Steuerberater** (see §6) if it's a filing month.
7. **Check the health metric:** founder hours/week. If creeping past 15, raise prices or pause intake — bookkeeping reflects the time budget, it doesn't override it.

Keep a clean separation: **a dedicated business bank account.** Never commingle with personal/salary money — it makes everything above slower and the Steuerberater more expensive.

## 2. Revenue & cost categories

**Revenue**
- Diagnostic fees
- Sprint fees (deposit / acceptance)
- Retainer (Operating Partner) fees

**Costs** (mirrors the financial model)
- **Subcontractor / delivery cost** (Year 2+, flat-rate)
- **Tooling** — AI APIs, automation platforms, hosting
- **Software / admin** — CRM, accounting, invoicing
- **Brand / marketing / content**
- **Professional fees** — Steuerberater, legal
- **Other** — bank fees, insurance, small equipment

Year-1 fixed monthly overhead is only a few hundred euros, so the **first Diagnostic covers months of cost** — this business is cash-positive from the first invoice.

---

## 3. Tax provisioning — set aside from day one

The side income **stacks on top of salaried income** and can push into higher marginal brackets. Provision conservatively so a tax bill is never a cash shock.

| Provision | Set aside (working default) | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **VAT (USt)** | **19% of every net invoice** (if charging USt) | You collect it for the state; it is **not your money**. Hold it for the Voranmeldung. |
| **Income tax (ESt + solidarity)** | **~35–42% of profit** | Side income on top of a salary is taxed at your top marginal rate. Conservative is safer. |

> Practical rule: on a €12,000 net Sprint invoice charged with 19% USt, the gross €14,280 lands in the account, but **€2,280 is VAT (not yours)** and you should reserve roughly **€4,000–5,000** of the net for income tax. Treat only the remainder as spendable. Confirm exact rates with your Steuerberater.

Hold provisions in a **separate tax sub-account** so the operating balance never lies to you.

---

## 4. Reserves

- **Tax reserve** — VAT + income-tax provisions (above), ring-fenced.
- **Operating buffer** — target **3 months of fixed costs**. Trivially small in Year 1; grows with subcontractor cost in Years 2–3.
- **Bad-debt cushion** — low risk thanks to upfront deposits, but keep the final-half receivable in mind on every open Sprint.

Because the business is bootstrapped and asset-light, reserves are about **smoothing**, not survival. No debt, no raise — *raise prices, not money*.

---

## 5. Simple cashflow forecast method

A rolling **3-month, invoice-driven** forecast is enough at this scale.

1. List **expected inflows** by date: signed deposits, scheduled acceptance halves, monthly retainers (use net-14 due dates, not optimistic ones).
2. List **expected outflows**: subcontractor invoices, tooling/software renewals, the **quarterly tax/VAT payment dates**.
3. **Running balance** = opening balance + inflows − outflows, week by week.
4. Flag any week the balance dips below the operating buffer — adjust by accelerating a deposit or deferring a discretionary cost.

Keep it in one sheet or, better, generated by the automation stack from the AR/AP data (see `docs/automation/`).

## 6. What to hand the Steuerberater

Hand over, ideally monthly or quarterly:
- All **issued invoices** (sequential, §14-compliant).
- All **received invoices / receipts** for costs (with VAT shown — needed for input-tax deduction).
- **Bank statements** for the business account.
- A **categorised transaction list** (the §2 buckets).
- Notes on anything unusual (credits, reverse-charge EU invoices, the Diagnostic-credit mechanism).

The cleaner and more structured the hand-off, the lower the fee. Your SOP/template discipline applies to your own books.

## 7. EÜR vs. Bilanz

- **EÜR (Einnahmenüberschussrechnung)** — simple cash-basis income-surplus statement. Available to **Freiberufler and small Einzelunternehmen** below the size thresholds. **This is almost certainly Lean-AI's regime** in Years 1–3: asset-light, service-only, well within limits.
- **Bilanz (double-entry accounting + balance sheet)** — mandatory for **GmbH/UG** regardless of size, and for Einzelunternehmen/Gewerbe once they exceed the turnover/profit thresholds (broadly **>€800k turnover or >€80k profit** — confirm current figures).

> Implication: stay on **EÜR** while a side business. Revisit if you incorporate (UG/GmbH) at the Year-3 scale decision (see `docs/governance/11-okrs-and-roadmap.md`) — incorporation forces Bilanz and higher accounting cost; weigh that against liability protection with your Steuerberater.

## 8. Key dates & deadlines (general)

| Item | Typical timing |
|---|---|
| **Umsatzsteuer-Voranmeldung** (VAT advance return) | Monthly or quarterly, by the **10th** of the following period (Dauerfristverlängerung can add a month) |
| **Income-tax prepayments (ESt-Vorauszahlung)** | Quarterly: **10 Mar / 10 Jun / 10 Sep / 10 Dec** |
| **Annual tax return + EÜR / USt-Jahreserklärung** | After year-end; deadline extended when filed via a Steuerberater |
| **Invoice retention** | **10 years** |

Put every recurring date in the calendar with a reminder; better, let the automation stack surface them. Exact obligations depend on your registration — confirm with your Steuerberater.
