# Lean-AI — Quote / Estimate (Angebot) Template

*A fill-in Angebot that signals premium, fixes scope, and closes cleanly.*

> **General information, not legal advice.** Whether an Angebot is legally binding depends on its wording — read §3 carefully and confirm with a professional. All prices are **net of VAT**; VAT is shown per the posture in `50-invoicing-process.md`.

---

## 1. How to use this template

- One Angebot per engagement. Replace every `[BRACKET]`.
- Keep it short. A premium offer is **confident and concrete**, not a 12-page proposal.
- Always reference it later on the invoice (e.g. "per Angebot AG-2026-014").
- Numbering: `AG-YYYY-NNN`, separate from invoice numbers.

## 3. Angebot vs. binding offer (read before sending)

Under German law, an offer (Angebot) is in principle **binding once it reaches the recipient** — they can accept it and form a contract on those terms.

To keep flexibility (e.g. WIP/start-date availability), mark the Angebot as **freibleibend** ("non-binding / subject to confirmation") and add a **validity date (gültig bis)**:

- **`freibleibend` / "without engagement"** — you are not bound until you confirm the client's acceptance. Useful when your start slot depends on the WIP limit.
- **`gültig bis [date]`** — a clear expiry (e.g. 14–30 days). After it lapses, the Angebot is void and you re-quote at current prices. This also creates healthy urgency, consistent with the scarcity of a WIP-limited studio.
- **Without these words**, a clean Angebot can bind you the moment the client says yes — fine if you're certain of capacity and price, risky otherwise.

> Recommendation: keep every Angebot **freibleibend with a validity date**, then make acceptance real by **issuing the deposit invoice** — the deposit clearing is the unambiguous start signal. This protects the WIP limit and the part-time time budget while still feeling decisive and premium. Confirm wording with a professional for your situation.
