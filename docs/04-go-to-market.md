# Lean-AI — Go-to-Market & 90-Day Launch Plan

*Founder-led, content-and-network motion. No paid ads in Year 1.*

The goal of GTM for a premium, part-time business is **not volume — it is authority and trust that convert a small number of high-value clients.** Every tactic below is chosen to (a) build standing as a thought/network leader, and (b) drive one specific action: *book the Diagnostic / a fit call.*

---

## 1. Positioning the funnel

```
   AUTHORITY (content + network)
        │   you become the obvious person for AI + Lean in the Mittelstand
        ▼
   FIT CALL (free, 30 min)
        │   qualify, diagnose pain, recommend honestly
        ▼
   THE DIAGNOSTIC (€4,500, paid)   ◀── the real "sales process"
        │   quantified roadmap + business case
        ▼
   AUTOMATION SPRINT (€12,000)
        │   the build; Diagnostic fee credited
        ▼
   OPERATING PARTNER (€3,500/mo)   ── the compounding engine
```

The paid Diagnostic is the linchpin: it filters out non-serious buyers, pays for the sales effort, and gives you the information to scope the build at full margin.

---

## 2. The three channels (in priority order)

### Channel 1 — Warm network (fastest path to revenue)
Your existing relationships already trust you; that is the scarcest asset in B2B services.
- Build a list of 30–50 people: former colleagues, SME owners/leaders you know, peers in operations/quality/IT.
- Reach out personally (not a broadcast). Frame: *"I've started Lean-AI — I help SMEs turn AI hype into running systems. Do you know a team drowning in a repetitive process?"*
- Ask for **introductions**, not sales meetings. Referrals convert far better than cold outreach.

### Channel 2 — Authority content ("network leadership")
One substantial artifact per week. Consistency beats volume.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn primary (where DACH SME decision-makers are), plus a simple article archive on the site later.
- **Format:** short case-style posts and teardown threads — *"Here's a 4-step way a 40-person company cut invoice processing from 3 days to 20 minutes."*
- **Signature move — be your own case study:** publish how *you* run Lean-AI on its own AI systems ("how I deliver a premium service in 12 hrs/week"). This is your single strongest, most defensible content angle.
- **Themes to rotate:** the Mittelstand AI gap; Lean-before-automation; servant leadership and adoption; AI data-safety for SMEs; real before/after metrics.

### Channel 3 — Selective partnerships
- Steuerberater, IT/MSP providers, and boutique management consultants serve the same SMEs but don't build AI systems — they are natural referral partners.
- Offer a clean referral arrangement and co-branded Diagnostics.

> **Explicitly out of scope for Year 1:** paid ads, SEO grind, cold-email blasts, marketplaces. They don't fit a premium, time-constrained founder and dilute the brand.

## 3. Sales approach

- **The fit call is a diagnosis, not a pitch.** Listen for a painful, repetitive, high-volume process with a quantifiable cost. If it's not there, say so honestly — that honesty is itself premium marketing.
- **Always recommend the Diagnostic** as the next step (never a free proposal-writing marathon).
- **Anchor on value:** "If this process costs you ~€X/year in time, a €12k Sprint that removes 70% of it pays back in under a quarter."
- **Use scarcity truthfully:** limited engagements per quarter (it's real — it's the WIP limit).
- **Contract & deposit before work:** Diagnostic paid 100% up front; Sprint 50% up front.

## 4. The 90-day launch plan

### Phase 0 — Foundation (Weeks 1–2)
- [ ] Get **written employer permission** for the side activity.
- [ ] Register the business; engage a Steuerberater; decide VAT posture.
- [ ] Website live (done — this repo); set up business email `hello@lean-ai.com`.
- [ ] Contract, proposal, invoice templates; payment method (Stripe/bank).
- [ ] Stand up the **internal AI delivery toolkit** (intake → findings → SOPs → proposals).
- [ ] Write the one-page Diagnostic spec and the Sprint scope template.

### Phase 1 — Authority & pipeline (Weeks 3–6)
- [ ] Publish content artifact #1; commit to weekly cadence.
- [ ] Personally contact 30–50 warm-network people; ask for intros.
- [ ] Book 8–12 fit calls.
- [ ] Goal: **1–2 paid Diagnostics signed.**

### Phase 2 — First builds (Weeks 7–12)
- [ ] Deliver Diagnostic(s); convert ≥1 to an Automation Sprint.
- [ ] Deliver the first Sprint; capture **before/after metrics** rigorously.
- [ ] Turn it into the first **case study** (anonymised if needed).
- [ ] Approach 2–3 referral partners.
- [ ] Goal by day 90: **1 Sprint delivered, 1 case study, ≥3 Diagnostics in or done, first retainer in conversation.**

## 5. Metrics that matter (and the ones that don't)

**Track:**
- Fit calls booked / month
- Diagnostic conversion rate (calls → paid Diagnostic)
- Sprint conversion rate (Diagnostic → Sprint)
- Retainer attach rate (Sprint → retainer) — target ≥40%
- Effective hourly rate per engagement (guards the time budget)
- Founder hours/week (the health metric — if it creeps past 15, raise prices or pause intake)

**Ignore:** follower counts, impressions, website visits in isolation. Authority is a means to qualified fit calls, nothing else.
