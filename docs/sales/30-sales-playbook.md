# Lean-AI — Sales Playbook

*How we turn a conversation into a built, owned system — honestly, and at full margin.*

This is the operating manual for selling Lean-AI. It assumes the founder-led, network-and-content motion in `docs/04-go-to-market.md` and the voice rules in `docs/06-brand-and-positioning.md`. The whole process exists to drive one action: **book the paid Diagnostic** — our core sales mechanism and front door to everything else.

We don't sell advice. We build the system, prove it works, and hand you the keys. The sale should feel like the start of that — diagnosis first, never a pitch.

---

## 1. The funnel

```
AUTHORITY (content + warm network + referral partners)
     ▼
FIT CALL — free, 30 min        → diagnose pain, qualify, recommend honestly
     ▼
THE DIAGNOSTIC — €4,500, paid  → quantified roadmap + business case (the real sale)
     ▼
AUTOMATION SPRINT — €12,000    → the build; Diagnostic fee credited
     ▼
OPERATING PARTNER — €3,500/mo  → the compounding engine; quarterly minimum
```

The paid Diagnostic is the linchpin. It filters non-serious buyers, pays for the sales effort, and gives us the information to scope a build at full margin. We do **not** write free, speculative proposals for the build — the Diagnostic *is* the scoping work, and it is paid.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

A client is a strong fit when **most** of these are true:

- **Size:** 10–250 employees; SME/Mittelstand in DACH/EU.
- **A painful, repetitive, high-volume process** with a quantifiable cost (hours, errors, delay, headcount pressure). This is the single most important signal.
- **Decision-maker access:** owner, MD/Geschäftsführer, or a department head with budget.
- **Curious but stuck on AI:** they know it matters, they lack the team to execute.
- **Can fund the work:** a €12k Sprint is a rounding error, not a bet-the-company decision.
- **Values ownership:** wants a system the team runs, not a dependency.
- **Decent data hygiene:** processes exist in *some* system (even spreadsheets), not pure tribal memory.

### Disqualifiers (walk away, or say "not yet")

- Wants advice/a deck, not a built system. (We're not a consultancy.)
- Looking for the cheapest option or a freelancer; price-shopping on hours.
- No identifiable process with a real cost — "we just want to do something with AI."
- No budget authority and no path to it.
- Wants it "by next week" with no scope discipline. (We don't sell fast; we sell done.)
- A broken process they want automated as-is without simplifying first.
- Wants us on-site full-time / staff augmentation.
- Regulated edge cases we can't responsibly serve solo (e.g. clinical, safety-critical) — refer out honestly.

When someone is a disqualifier, **tell them**, kindly and specifically. The candor is premium marketing and it protects the WIP limit.

## 3. The consultative, honest approach

1. **The fit call is a diagnosis, not a pitch.** Listen 70% of the time. Find the one process worth fixing.
2. **Quantify before you recommend.** No numbers, no recommendation. "How many times a week? How long each? Who does it?"
3. **Recommend the smallest right thing.** If the Diagnostic isn't warranted yet, say so and stay in touch. Never upsell past the real need.
4. **Anchor on value, never hours.** "If this costs ~€X/year, a €12k Sprint that removes 70% pays back in under a quarter."
5. **Say no out loud.** Telling a prospect we can't help them builds more trust than any case study.
6. **Outcomes, not activity.** Every commitment is framed as a result you'll own, with a number attached.

Language: *transformation partner, operating partner, operating system, outcomes not hours, done-with-you then done-for-you, the keys.* Never: *consultant, freelancer, cheap, fast, Zaps.*

## 4. Handling objections

Acknowledge → reframe to value/ownership → restate the next step (usually the Diagnostic).

**"It's expensive / can you do it cheaper?"**
> "I understand — and we're deliberately not the cheapest option. The Diagnostic is €4,500, and every euro is credited toward the Sprint if you proceed, so it's not a sunk cost. The question isn't the price of the work; it's the cost of the process staying as it is. You told me it's eating roughly [X hours/week]. At that rate, doing nothing costs you ~€[Y] this year. We fix that for a fixed price, and you own the result."

**"We'll just do it internally / with our IT team."**
> "Often you can — and if the Diagnostic shows that, I'll tell you plainly. But two things usually stall internal builds: the process isn't simplified first, so you automate the mess; and nobody owns adoption, so it dies after launch. Our whole method is Lean-first, then build, then hand over a system your team runs. We're done-with-you, then done-for-you — and then you have the keys, not us."

**"Is our data safe? / We can't send data to AI."**
> "Right concern, and it's exactly why we're not a 'plug-in-ChatGPT' shop. We design for data residency and minimisation up front — we scope what data is actually needed, keep sensitive data in your environment where required, and choose models and hosting accordingly. Data handling is part of the Diagnostic, in writing, before any build."

**"Is AI even reliable / safe for real work?"**
> "AI is a component, not magic. We don't point a model at a critical process and hope. We simplify the process with Lean, automate the parts that are safe to automate, keep a human in the loop where judgment matters, and instrument everything so you can see it working. You sign off on what's automated and what isn't."

**"Now's not the right time."**
> "Fair. Two honest questions: is the process you described getting cheaper or more expensive as you grow? And what would have to be true for the timing to be right? If it's budget timing, the Diagnostic is a low, creditable first step we can run now and have the build ready to greenlight when you are. If it's genuinely later, I'll check back in [timeframe] — no pressure."

**"Send me a proposal first."** (for a Sprint, before a Diagnostic)
> "Happy to put the *Diagnostic* in writing today. I won't quote the build yet, because guessing the scope would either over- or under-serve you. The Diagnostic exists precisely to scope the build properly — and its fee is credited toward it."

## 5. Scarcity — truthful, via the WIP limit

We run **one active build at a time, plus up to ~2 retainers**, with Diagnostics slotted between (see `docs/03-financial-model.md`). That is a real constraint, not a tactic. Use it plainly:

> "I take on a limited number of builds at once so each one gets full attention — that's how the quality holds. My next Sprint slot is [month]. The Diagnostic isn't slot-constrained, so we can start there and have you ready to book the build."

Never manufacture fake urgency. Real scarcity, stated calmly, is enough.

## 6. Pipeline stages & CRM fields

### Stages

| # | Stage | Entry condition | Exit / next |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | **Lead** | Identified or inbound; ICP plausible | Fit call booked |
| 1 | **Fit call booked** | Calendar invite accepted | Call held |
| 2 | **Qualified** | Scorecard ≥ threshold; pain quantified | Diagnostic proposed |
| 3 | **Diagnostic proposed** | One-page Diagnostic proposal sent | Signed + paid |
| 4 | **Diagnostic won** | Contract signed, 100% paid | Diagnostic delivered |
| 5 | **Diagnostic delivered** | Roadmap + business case handed over | Sprint proposed |
| 6 | **Sprint proposed** | Sprint proposal sent (Diagnostic credited) | Signed + deposit |
| 7 | **Sprint won** | Contract signed, 50% deposit cleared | Build → handover |
| 8 | **Retainer conversation** | Sprint delivered; attach pitch made | Retainer signed |

### CRM fields (minimum)

- Company, size (FTE), industry, location
- Contact name, role, decision authority (Y/N/Influencer)
- Source (warm intro / content / referral partner — name it)
- **The process** (one line: the painful, repetitive, high-volume process)
- **Quantified cost** (€/year estimate)
- Budget signal (can fund €12k? Y/N/Unknown)
- Timeline (when do they want it running?)
- Stage + stage entry date
- Next action + due date
- Scorecard total (see file 31)
- Diagnostic credit on file (€) — for Sprint pricing
- Notes / data-sensitivity flags

## 7. Definitions of Done (per stage)

- **Lead → Fit call:** time confirmed, agenda set ("30 min, I'll ask about your processes, you'll leave with a clear next step or an honest no").
- **Fit call → Qualified:** scorecard completed in CRM; the process and its €/year cost written down; budget/authority/timeline captured.
- **Qualified → Diagnostic proposed:** one-page Diagnostic proposal sent within 24h, value-framed, with a clear price (€4,500) and the credit note.
- **Diagnostic proposed → Won:** contract signed and **100% paid** before work starts.
- **Diagnostic delivered:** roadmap + quantified business case + prioritised opportunities handed over; Sprint recommendation made.
- **Sprint proposed → Won:** contract signed, **50% deposit cleared** before any build work begins.
- **Sprint delivered → Retainer:** before/after metrics captured; attach conversation held; offer made.

## 8. Follow-up cadence

Premium ≠ pushy. Persistent, value-led, never desperate.

- **After a fit call:** recap email same day with the quantified pain and the recommended next step (proposal attached if qualified).
- **After a proposal:** check-in at **day 3**, **day 7**, **day 14**. Each touch adds something (a relevant example, a data-safety note, a slot update) — never just "any update?".
- **No response after day 14:** one clean "closing the loop" message, then move to **Not now** and the re-engagement cadence (file 34).
- **Not now / parked:** light-touch every 6–8 weeks via content + a personal note. Re-engage when their trigger (growth, hire, deadline) likely fired.
- **Post-Diagnostic, no Sprint yet:** follow up tied to the business case — "the €[Y]/year we quantified is still running; here's the slot."

All follow-up is signed by the founder, plain-text-feeling, from `hello@lean-ai.com`.

## 9. The metrics that govern this playbook

(From `docs/04-go-to-market.md` — track these, ignore vanity numbers.)

- Fit calls booked / month
- Call → paid Diagnostic conversion
- Diagnostic → Sprint conversion
- **Retainer attach rate — target ≥40%**
- Effective hourly rate per engagement
- Founder hours/week (health metric — past 15, raise prices or pause intake)

If the funnel is full but hours are creeping, the answer is **raise prices**, not work more.
