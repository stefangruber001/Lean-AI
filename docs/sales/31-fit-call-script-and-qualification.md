# Lean-AI — Fit-Call Script & Qualification

*A 30-minute diagnosis that ends in an honest recommendation — usually the Diagnostic.*

The fit call is free, lasts 30 minutes, and is **a diagnosis, not a pitch**. The goal is to find one painful, repetitive, high-volume process with a quantifiable cost — and either recommend the Diagnostic or tell the prospect honestly that we're not the right fit. Use the language in `docs/06-brand-and-positioning.md`. Listen ~70% of the time.

Have the CRM scorecard (Section 4) open during the call.

---

## 1. The 30-minute structure

| Minutes | Phase | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0–3 | Opening & frame | Set expectations, lower the pressure |
| 3–15 | Discovery | Find the painful process |
| 15–22 | Quantify | Put a € number on it |
| 22–26 | Qualify | Budget, authority, timeline |
| 26–30 | Recommend & close | Diagnostic, honest no, or park |

---

## 2. The script

### Opening & frame (0–3 min)

> "Thanks for the time. Here's how I'd like to use the next half hour: I'll ask about how your team actually works day to day — where the repetitive, time-eating stuff lives. By the end you'll get a clear, honest read from me: either a specific next step, or 'this isn't worth your money yet.' I won't pitch you. Sound good?"

> "Quick context on us, in one line: Lean-AI builds AI-native operating systems for companies your size. We don't sell advice — we remove waste with Lean, automate the rest with AI agents, and hand your team a system they own. But that's only worth doing if there's a real process behind it, which is what I want to find today."

### Discovery — find the painful process (3–15 min)

Open broad, then narrow to one process. Take notes verbatim where you can.

- "Walk me through what your team spends the most *repetitive* time on — the stuff people complain about."
- "Where do things get stuck, re-keyed, or chased? Where do errors creep in?"
- "If you cloned your best operations person, what boring work would you hand them first?"
- "Which process touches the most people or the most volume — invoices, orders, onboarding, quotes, support tickets, reporting?"
- "What have you already tried to fix it? What happened?"
- "Who does this work today, and what could they be doing instead?"

Listen for: high frequency, manual repetition, copy-paste between systems, delay, error/rework, a person whose time is being burned. **That's the candidate process.** Mirror it back:

> "So if I've got it right: [process] happens [N times/week], takes [team] roughly [time] each, and it's [late / error-prone / blocking X]. Is that the one that, if it ran itself, would change your week?"

### Quantify — put a € on it (15–22 min)

No number, no recommendation.

- "How many times does this happen — per day or per week?"
- "How long does each one take, end to end, including the chasing?"
- "How many people touch it?"
- "What does a mistake here cost you — rework, a late delivery, an unhappy customer?"
- "If this disappeared tomorrow, what would that free your team to do?"

Do the math out loud:

> "So that's roughly [N/week] × [hours each] × [people] ≈ [X hours/week]. Over a year, call it ~€[Y] in loaded time — before we count the errors and the delay. Does that feel about right, or conservative?"

(Let them adjust the number. Their number is more persuasive than yours.)

### Qualify — budget, authority, timeline (22–26 min)

Conversational, not an interrogation.

- **Authority:** "If we found a fix worth doing, who decides to go ahead — just you, or others involved?"
- **Budget:** "To set expectations: a build to fix something like this is typically a €12k fixed-price Sprint, and it starts with a €4,500 Diagnostic that's credited toward it. Is that the right order of magnitude for a problem costing you ~€[Y]/year?"
- **Timeline:** "When would you want this actually running — this quarter, next? Is anything forcing the timing?"

### Recommend & close (26–30 min)

**If qualified — recommend the Diagnostic:**

> "Here's my honest read. You've got a real, expensive process and it's exactly the kind of thing we build out. I don't want to guess the solution, so the right next step is the Diagnostic: over 2–3 weeks I map the process, simplify it, and hand you a quantified roadmap and business case — what to build, the expected payback, and how the data is handled. It's €4,500, and if you go ahead with the Sprint, that fee is fully credited. Worst case, you keep a roadmap you own. Shall I send it over today so you can look at it properly?"

Then: confirm the decision-maker, agree a date to review, and send the one-page Diagnostic proposal within 24 hours.

**If not a fit — the honest no:**

> "I'll be straight with you: I don't think you need us yet. [Reason — e.g. the process isn't standard enough to automate safely / the cost doesn't justify the build / you'd be better simplifying this internally first]. If [trigger] changes, come back — and in the meantime I'll keep sharing what I learn. I'd rather lose the work than sell you something you don't need."

**If interested but not now — park it:**

> "Sounds like the problem is real but the timing isn't. Let's do this: I'll check back in [6–8 weeks] when [trigger] is closer. If anything heats up before then, the Diagnostic is a low first step we can start quickly."

## 4. Qualification scorecard

Score each line 0–2. Record the total in the CRM. **Threshold to recommend the Diagnostic: ≥ 9 / 14, with no zero on a starred (★) line.**

| # | Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 ★ | **Painful repetitive process** | None identified | Vague / one-off | Clear, high-volume, named |
| 2 ★ | **Quantifiable cost** | Can't / won't quantify | Rough guess | Credible €/year, owned by them |
| 3 | **Volume / frequency** | Occasional | Weekly | Daily / continuous |
| 4 ★ | **Decision authority** | No access | Influencer only | Decision-maker on the call |
| 5 | **Budget fit (€12k Sprint)** | Out of reach | Stretch | Comfortable |
| 6 | **Timeline** | "Someday" | This year | This / next quarter, with a driver |
| 7 | **Wants a built, owned system** | Wants advice/deck | Mixed | Yes — values ownership |

Add notes for: data-sensitivity flags, who else must approve, and the exact words they used for the pain (use them in the proposal).

## 5. Red flags (raise the bar or walk)

- **Price-shopping on hours** or comparing us to a freelancer — wrong category; we sell outcomes, not hours.
- **"We just want to do something with AI"** — no process, no number. Not yet.
- **Wants the broken process automated as-is** — we don't automate the mess; Lean comes first.
- **No path to a decision-maker** — the deal will stall.
- **Wants it next week** with no scope discipline — we don't sell fast.
- **"Send me a Sprint quote first"** — redirect to the Diagnostic; never free-scope a build.
- **Data they legally can't move and won't discuss** — flag; may be a polite referral.
- **Chaos with no system of record** — even spreadsheets are fine, but pure tribal memory isn't buildable yet.

When two or more red flags show, lean toward the honest no or a park — it protects the WIP limit and the brand.
