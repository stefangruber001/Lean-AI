# Lean-AI — Outreach Sequences

*Ready-to-send copy for the warm-network and referral motion. Personal, plain, never a broadcast.*

All outreach is founder-signed, plain-text-feeling, and asks for **introductions and fit calls — not the sale**. No cold-email blasts, no paid ads (per `docs/04-go-to-market.md`). Voice per `docs/06-brand-and-positioning.md`: confident, plain, honest. Never *cheap, fast, freelancer, Zaps.* The two most important messages — the warm-network announcement and the referral-partner outreach — appear in both English and German.

Replace every `[bracket]`. Keep it short. One ask per message.

---

## 1. Warm-network announcement

The single highest-leverage message. Send individually, never as a list email.

**English**

>

>

We help SMEs turn AI from hype into operations that actually run: we remove the waste with Lean, automate the rest with AI agents, and hand the team a system they own. If someone comes to mind, a quick intro would mean a lot. Happy to give them a straight, no-pressure read on whether it's even worth fixing.
>

## 4. The 4-touch follow-up sequence (LinkedIn + email)

Use after a connection, a content reply, or a stalled conversation. Each touch **adds value** — never "just checking in." Stop and move to re-engagement after touch 4.

**Touch 1 — Day 0 (LinkedIn connect / reply)**
> Good to connect, [Name]. I saw [specific thing — their post / role / company news]. I work with SMEs on taking repetitive manual processes off the team with AI. No agenda — just glad to be connected.

**Touch 2 — Day 4 (LinkedIn message or email)**
> [Name], a thought after seeing [their context]: most teams I meet are losing more time to [process type] than they realise — often [N hours/week]. I wrote a short breakdown of how one company cut [process] from [X] to [Y]: [link]. Might be relevant.

**Touch 3 — Day 9 (email — the soft ask)**
> Hi [Name], rather than keep sharing links — would a 30-minute call be useful? I'd ask about where your team loses the most repetitive time and give you an honest read on whether it's worth fixing. No pitch. If now's not the moment, just say and I'll keep my distance.

**Touch 4 — Day 16 (email — close the loop)**
> Hi [Name], I'll stop here so I'm not cluttering your inbox. If [process] ever becomes the thing you want off your plate, I'm one message away. Wishing you and [Company] a strong quarter.

(Then → re-engagement cadence below.)

## 5. Re-engagement message (parked / went quiet)

Send 6–8 weeks after a stall, ideally tied to a likely trigger (year-end, growth, a hire, a deadline).

> Subject: Still on my mind — [Process]
>

>
> A while back we talked about [the process] costing your team roughly [N hours/week]. With [trigger — year-end / the new hires / the busy season] coming up, it came to mind again.

>
> Want me to send it over, or should I check back later in the year?
>
> — [Founder]
