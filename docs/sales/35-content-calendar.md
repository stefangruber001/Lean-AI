# Lean-AI — Content Calendar & Authority System

*One substantial artifact a week. Consistency over volume. Authority that converts a few high-value clients.*

Content is not marketing reach — it's **network leadership**. Its only job is to make the founder the obvious person for AI + Lean in the Mittelstand, and to drive one action: *book a fit call → book the Diagnostic.* LinkedIn is the primary channel (where DACH SME decision-makers are). Voice per `docs/06-brand-and-positioning.md`: confident, plain, concrete, honest. Never *cheap, fast, Zaps,* buzzword soup.

## 2. Formats

- **Process teardown** — "Here's how a 40-person company cut [process] from [3 days] to [20 min], in 4 steps." (Highest performer.)
- **Before/after metric post** — one chart or one number, plainly explained.
- **Honest take** — a contrarian, candid view ("Most 'AI strategies' fail because nobody simplified first").
- **Build-in-public** — the "be your own case study" thread: how Lean-AI runs on its own systems.
- **Field note** — a short observation from a real conversation (anonymised).
- **Mini-guide** — a reusable checklist or framework readers can apply themselves.

Each post ends with a soft, consistent CTA — never "DM me for a free audit." Examples: *"If your team loses time to something like this, I'm happy to give an honest read — no pitch."*

## 4. The "be your own case study" angle (signature move)

This is the strongest, most defensible content angle — nobody can copy your lived proof. Show, with specifics and screenshots where safe:

- The agents and Lean steps behind running a premium service in ~12 hrs/week.
- A real before/after from your *own* operations (intake → findings → SOPs → proposals).
- The honest tradeoffs — what you keep human, where AI fails, what you'd never automate.

Rule: only claim what you can actually show. Honesty is the marketing.

## 5. Repurposing workflow

One artifact, many cuts. Weekly loop:

1. **Write the LinkedIn post/thread** (the primary artifact).
2. **Pull a pull-quote graphic** (one line, brand colours — Ink + Gold) for visual variety.
3. **Save it to the article archive** on `lean-ai.com` (builds an evergreen library).
4. **Reuse the strongest line** as a one-liner in outreach (see file 34) and the recap email.
5. **Bank the best teardowns** as raw material for case studies (file 36) and the deck (file 37).
6. **Quarterly:** compile the top 3 posts into a short "field notes" PDF for referral partners to forward.

Batch writing: draft 3–4 weeks ahead in one sitting to fit the 10–15 hr/week constraint.

## 6. Metrics that matter (and the ones that don't)

**Track (the funnel, not the applause):**
- **Fit calls booked** that name content as the source — the only outcome that counts.
- **Saves / meaningful comments / DMs** from ICP-fit people (decision-makers at SMEs).
- **Profile visits → connection requests** from target roles.
- **Conversion: content touch → fit call → Diagnostic** (tag the source in the CRM).

**Ignore:** follower count, impressions, likes in isolation. Authority is a means to qualified fit calls — nothing else (per `docs/04-go-to-market.md`).

**Cadence check:** if posting slips below weekly, cut scope elsewhere before cutting content — consistency is the whole strategy.
