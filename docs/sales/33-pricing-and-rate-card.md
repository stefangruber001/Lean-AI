## Discount & credit policy

- **Diagnostic → Sprint credit:** the full Diagnostic fee (€4,500) is credited toward an Automation Sprint booked within **90 days**. This is the only standing "discount," and it's structural — the Diagnostic is a paid scoping step, not a profit centre.
- **No hourly discounting, no haggling.** We hold price and adjust scope. If budget is tight, we narrow the scope to the highest-payback slice — we don't cut the rate.
- **No free trials, no free pilots, no speculative proposals for the build.** The Diagnostic is the paid pilot.
- **Price increases:** list prices rise ~10–15% once at ≥80% capacity (per `docs/03-financial-model.md`). Active retainers are honoured to the next quarterly renewal.

## How to talk about price

1. **Anchor on the cost of inaction, not our fee.** "The process costs you ~€[Y]/year. The question is what *not* fixing it costs." (See `docs/sales/31-fit-call-script-and-qualification.md`.)
2. **Lead with the Diagnostic — it's small and creditable.** "€4,500 to know exactly what to build and whether it pays back, fully credited if you proceed." Removes the risk from the bigger decision.
3. **Hold price; flex scope.** Never discount the rate. If budget is tight, ship the highest-payback slice first.
4. **Frame outcomes, never hours.** "A fixed price for a system you own and a measured result" — never a day rate.
5. **Use real scarcity calmly.** One active build at a time; book the slot early. Never fake urgency.
6. **Be honest about ongoing cost.** Name the retainer at handover — operations that compound are worth paying for, and saying so up front builds trust (target attach ≥40%).
7. **Words matter.** *Investment, fixed price, outcomes, the keys* — never *cheap, fast, hourly, freelancer.*
