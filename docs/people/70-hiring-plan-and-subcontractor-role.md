# Lean-AI — Hiring Plan & First Subcontractor Role

*When to add a second pair of hands, who they are, and how the SOP library makes them effective on day one.*

> **General information, not legal advice.** Subcontractor classification (Scheinselbstständigkeit) is a real German legal risk — see §5 and confirm the arrangement with a lawyer / Steuerberater before signing.

---

## 1. When to make the first hire

**Rule: hire to relieve capacity you've already sold — never to create capacity you can't yet sell.**

Trigger conditions (per `docs/05-operating-model.md` and the financial model):
- **≥2 stable retainers** reliably funding the cost, **or**
- a **Sprint pipeline you cannot serve inside the WIP limit** (one active build + ~2 retainers).

This lands in **Year 2** in the base case, where the model already books **–€36k** of subcontractor cost against ~€200k revenue. The first hire **doubles build/retainer capacity without doubling founder hours** — and trades a little margin (87% → 72%) for growth and time-protection. Do not hire earlier; in Year 1 the founder delivers everything and the business is cash-positive precisely because delivery cost is ~€0.

---

## 2. Why a flat-rate subcontractor (not an hourly employee)

- **Fixed cost, fixed scope.** Pay a flat rate per defined deliverable or a monthly retainer for a defined queue — mirrors how Lean-AI sells. No hourly drift, no payroll/social-contribution overhead, no employment-law commitment for a part-time business.
- **Time-protective.** A flat rate gives the sub the incentive to be efficient and removes the founder from time-policing.
- **Reversible.** A two-way door: scale up or down with demand without an employee's protections and obligations.
- **Premium stays in-house.** The founder keeps the relationship, diagnosis, and method (the high-value, high-judgement work); the sub takes the templatable execution.

> Caution: a flat-rate **subcontractor** is genuinely self-employed only if the arrangement is real (see §5). Don't recreate an employment relationship under a freelance label.

---

## 3. Role spec — First Subcontractor (Delivery / Build)

**Title:** Delivery & Automation Subcontractor (flat-rate, project-based)

**Mission:** Execute defined build, integration, documentation, and QA tasks from Lean-AI's SOP/template library to the premium standard — so the founder's hours go further without lowering quality.

**Scope of work (IN scope)**
- **Build:** implement AI agents, automations, and integrations against real client cases, from a scoped brief and existing templates.
- **Integration & configuration:** connect tools/systems; configure dashboards and instrumentation.
- **Documentation:** write SOPs, runbooks, and handover docs to the library standard.
- **QA / testing:** test against real cases; human-in-the-loop checks; fix defects before the founder's review gate.
- **Template hygiene:** turn each build into a clean, reusable parameterised template (the compounding asset).

**Explicitly OUT of scope**
- **Client relationship / account ownership** — the founder owns all client communication.
- **Diagnosis / method / strategy** — value-stream mapping, opportunity prioritisation, and the "what to build" decision stay with the founder.
- **Pricing, scoping, and proposals.**
- **Final sign-off / handover to the client** — the founder runs the premium handover and servant-leadership training.

**Working norms**
- Async-first; works from written briefs and the template library.
- Self-employed: uses **own tools, own equipment, own time**, and ideally **serves other clients**.
- Every deliverable passes the **founder's QA gate** before a client sees it.

**Compensation:** flat rate per deliverable, or a fixed monthly amount for an agreed queue — invoiced to Lean-AI by the subcontractor (§14-compliant). No hourly billing.

## 4. Find, vet, and onboard

**Find**
- Warm network first; trusted referrals; specialist freelance communities. Premium work attracts via reputation, not job boards.

**Vet**
- Portfolio of *built, running* systems (not advice).
- **Paid trial task:** a real, scoped queue item delivered from an SOP. This is the single best signal — can they hit the standard from the library, with minimal hand-holding?
- Cultural fit: Lean mindset, documentation discipline, comfort with async and human-in-the-loop AI.

**Onboard**
- **The SOP / template library *is* the onboarding.** If a sub can deliver from it, the library is good enough to be the product; if they can't, fix the library. (This is the test described in `docs/05-operating-model.md`.)
- Start with the most templatable, least-judgement work (integrations, dashboards, documentation, QA).
- Sign the **flat-rate contract + NDA/DPA** before any client data is shared.

## 5. Scheinselbstständigkeit caution

German authorities can reclassify a "freelancer" as a de-facto employee (**Scheinselbstständigkeit**) — triggering back-payment of social contributions, taxes, and employer obligations. To keep the arrangement genuinely independent:

- **Per-deliverable / project scope**, not fixed working hours or "presence."
- The sub **uses their own tools, equipment, and methods** and decides *how* the work is done.
- The sub should **work for other clients** and not be economically dependent on Lean-AI (a common red flag is a freelancer earning ~all income from one client).
- **No integration into a team structure**, no Lean-AI email/title implying employment, no instruction-and-control like a boss.
- Keep clean contracts and invoices.

> This is a real risk (see `docs/governance/12-risk-register.md`, #10). **Confirm the setup with a lawyer/Steuerberater before engaging the first subcontractor.**

## 6. Path to a small senior team (Year-3 scale scenario)

If the **Year-3 fork** (see `docs/governance/11-okrs-and-roadmap.md`) chooses *go full-time and scale*:

- Convert proven flat-rate subcontractor(s) into a **small senior team** — senior-only, no juniors, keeping the asset-light, high-margin model.
- The model's Year-3 delivery cost (~€78k against ~€320k revenue) already assumes meaningful subcontractor leverage; the team forms around people who have **already delivered to standard from the library**.
- Keep the founder on **relationship, diagnosis, and method**; delegate progressively more delivery and template ownership.
- Revisit **legal form** (UG/GmbH → Bilanz; see `docs/finance/51-cashflow-and-bookkeeping.md`) and employment vs. contractor mix with professional advice as headcount grows.
- Target the same productized model at €750k–1M — proven solo productized firms operate at this scale with small senior teams.

If the fork chooses *stay a side business*, **do not** build the team — hold one trusted subcontractor and let price, not headcount, absorb demand.
